Pandwani

Pandwani is a traditional performing art form from the Indian state of Chhattisgarh, particularly associated with the Gond tribal community. It is a vibrant and dynamic narrative folk tradition that revolves around the tales from the epic Mahabharata, particularly the stories related to the Pandavas. Pandwani is characterized by its unique blend of storytelling, music, and dramatic expression.



Origin

Pandwani has its roots in the oral traditions of the Gond community in Chhattisgarh. The term "Pandwani" itself means "the story of the Pandavas," reflecting its focus on the narratives from the Mahabharata.

Performance Style

Pandwani is performed by a solo artist known as the "Pandwani artist" or "Pandwaniya." The artist narrates the epic stories using a combination of song and dialogue, accompanied by rhythmic percussion instruments. The performer enacts various characters, using expressive gestures and body movements to bring the narratives to life.

Music and Instruments

The musical accompaniment in Pandwani is provided by traditional instruments such as the "kartaal" (cymbals) and the "manjira" (small hand cymbals). The artist often uses a tambura for melodic support. The rhythmic patterns and musicality add depth and emotion to the storytelling.

Themes

The primary themes of Pandwani revolve around the Mahabharata, with a focus on the trials and tribulations faced by the Pandavas. The narratives highlight moral and ethical dilemmas, valor, and the complexities of human relationships.

Expressive Storytelling

Pandwani involves a high level of expressiveness and dramatic storytelling. The Pandwaniya employs a variety of vocal techniques, ranging from melodic singing to dramatic recitation, to engage the audience and convey the emotional nuances of the stories.

Costumes

The Pandwani artist typically wears traditional attire, often adorned with colorful fabrics and jewelry. The costumes may vary based on the character being portrayed, helping the audience distinguish between different roles.
