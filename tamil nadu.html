Terukkuttu

Terukkuttu, meaning “street theatre,” is a vibrant form of traditional Tamil drama performed in open spaces. It blends storytelling, dance, music, and dramatic dialogue, often revolving around episodes from the Mahabharata, especially the story of Draupadi. Terukkuttu is deeply rooted in Tamil Nadu’s rural culture and spiritual expression.

Origin

Terukkuttu originated in Tamil Nadu as a folk theatre tradition to spread moral, spiritual, and social values through live performances in village streets and temple festivals.

Themes

Themes often center on episodes from the Mahabharata, especially the story of Draupadi, highlighting justice, honor, and dharma.

Performance

Terukkuttu is performed overnight in open areas with elaborate costumes, expressive gestures, Tamil songs, and strong dramatic narration.

Instruments

Musical instruments like harmonium, mridangam, and cymbals are used to accompany songs and heighten dramatic moments.

Costume

Performers wear bright makeup, headgear, and traditional attire that reflect the character they portray, often with symbolic face paint.

Cultural Role

Terukkuttu plays a key role in preserving Tamil oral literature, transmitting epics and values to rural audiences, especially during festivals.
